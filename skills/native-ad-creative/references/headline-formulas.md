# Headline Formulas

Fill-in-the-blank templates organized by psychological angle. Each formula includes the template, examples, and notes on why it works.

---

## Curiosity Gap Formulas

### The Hidden Cause

```
The reason why [common problem] keeps getting worse
```

**Examples:**
- "The reason why your joint pain keeps getting worse"
- "The reason why your energy crashes at 2pm"
- "The reason why your skin breaks out every winter"

**Why it works:** Implies the reader is making a mistake they don't know about. Curiosity + fear of loss = clicks.

---

### The Discovery

```
[Age] year old [location] [person] discovers [simple solution] for [problem]
```

**Examples:**
- "62 year old Texas grandmother discovers simple trick for stiff joints"
- "45 year old Ohio teacher discovers unusual method for deep sleep"

**Why it works:** Specific age creates relatability. Location builds trust. "Discovers" triggers curiosity. Simple language makes it accessible. Old school direct response — still outperforms anything clever.

---

### The Unfinished Sentence

Cut the headline mid-thought. Force the brain to complete it.

**Examples:**
- "If you have high blood pressure, quickly drink a glass of..."
- "If you have arthritis, please stop eating this..."
- "Anyone over 60: the risk hiding in plain sight is..."

**Why it works:** The Zeigarnik Effect — the brain cannot tolerate incomplete patterns. An unfinished sentence creates cognitive tension that can only be resolved by clicking.

---

### The Bizarre Connection

Link two things that don't obviously belong together.

**Examples:**
- "People are turning to gelatin for stubborn belly fat"
- "Cleanse your liver to lose belly fat"
- "This household item works better than collagen for wrinkles"

**Why it works:** The connection makes no sense. The brain flags it as new information, not advertising. You click because you need the logic to make sense.

---

### The Tiny Ask

Frame the solution as absurdly simple. The gap between the small input and big outcome is the hook.

**Examples:**
- "Constantly bloated and no idea why? This trick will deflate you in 9 days."
- "Understanding this simple trick about overeating made me throw out all sweets from the house. Now I lost 13lbs in 6 weeks."
- "This nut will help your cramping legs in less than 27mins."

**Why it works:** Most people have been burned by complicated solutions. A tiny ask (one trick, one food, one habit) feels risk-free. The brain thinks "what do I have to lose?" and clicks.

## Enemy Framing Formulas

### The Trusted Threat

```
Anyone Over [Age]: [Trusted Thing] Is A Risk Hiding In Plain Sight
```

**Examples:**
- "Anyone Over 60: Eggs Are A Risk Hiding In Plain Sight"
- "Anyone Over 50: This Breakfast Staple Is Silently Raising Your Blood Pressure"

**Why it works:** Turn something safe and familiar into a threat. That betrayal triggers emotion. Emotion triggers clicks. The brain cannot scroll past a threat without knowing if it applies.

---

### The Expert Warning

```
[Expert Title] Says: Please Stop [Doing Common Thing] If You Have [Condition]
```

**Examples:**
- "Rheumatologist Says: Please Stop Eating This Food If You Have Arthritis"
- "Cardiologist Warns: The Breakfast Habit That's Damaging Your Heart"

**Why it works:** "Please" changes everything. A doctor is personally worried about you. The authority is instant. The food is unnamed — you must click to find out.

---

### The Single Villain

```
[Condition Callout]: This One [Common Thing] Is [Negative Consequence]
```

**Examples:**
- "If you care about your blood sugar: this one food is spiking it daily"
- "Blood sugar is not from sweets! This one common ingredient is the real culprit"

**Why it works:** One enemy, not a list. A single target to blame is much more doable than 27 things you did wrong. The brain loves a single villain.

---

### The Contrarian Challenge

```
[Common Problem] Has Nothing To Do With [Accepted Cause]
```

**Examples:**
- "Prostate problems have nothing to do with age"
- "Your fatigue has nothing to do with sleep"
- "Blood sugar is not from sweets!"

**Why it works:** Challenges an accepted belief. Breaks an assumption and you own their attention. Confused people click to resolve the confusion.

## Authority / Third-Party Formulas

### The Investigation

```
We Tested the [Product] Going Viral With [Demographic]. Here's What Happened
```

**Examples:**
- "We Tested the Joint Supplement Going Viral With Seniors. Here's What Happened"
- "We Investigated the Sleep Method Trending With Night Shift Workers. Here's the Truth"

**Why it works:** Positions the offer as something investigated, not advertised. Feels editorial. Hits curiosity (what did they find?), trust (not the brand talking), and native context (feels like content).

---

### The Specialist Directive

```
[Specialist Title] Says: Do This for [Short Time] To [Relieve Specific Pain]
```

**Examples:**
- "Spine Specialist Says: Do This for 15min To Relieve Sciatica"
- "Dentist Goes Viral for Whitening Advice. Here's the 2-Step Method."

**Why it works:** Borrowed authority from a specialist. Micro-commitment (15 minutes, 2 steps — not a lifestyle change). Specific pain mapped to specific promise.

---

### The Reveal

```
[Expert Title] Reveals [Contrarian Claim About Trusted Product]
```

**Examples:**
- "Plastic Surgeon Reveals Why Collagen Is Making Wrinkles Worse"
- "Nutritionist Reveals the Real Reason Diets Stop Working After 40"
- "Pharmacist explains: The reason why most anti-migraine solutions don't work long term"

**Why it works:** Expert exposing something. You trust the authority AND you're curious about what's being hidden. Credibility + curiosity + pain.

---

### The Only One

```
[Expert Title] Reveals: The Only [Product] [They Recommend/Trust]
```

**Examples:**
- "Cardiologist reveals: the only olive oil he recommends"
- "Dermatologist reveals: the only sunscreen she actually uses"

**Why it works:** "Only" does all the work. Not "best." Not "favorite." The *only* one. Everything else is worse. Eliminates the need for research. Authority + scarcity in one sentence.

## Social Proof / FOMO Formulas

### The Movement

```
[Demographic] Are [Action]—And [Big Brand] Is Panicking
```

**Examples:**
- "Millennials Are Canceling Cable—And Comcast Is Panicking"
- "British Homeowners Are Claiming This Scheme—And Energy Companies Hate It"

**Why it works:** "People are turning to X" beats "You should try X." Make them feel like they're discovering a movement, not being pitched. Trends sell. Ads don't.

---

### The Skeptic Converted

```
She Was Skeptical. [Timeframe] Later, [Result].
```

**Examples:**
- "She Was Skeptical. 90 Days Later, Down 3 Dress Sizes."
- "He Didn't Believe It Would Work. 2 Weeks Later, Sleeping Through the Night."

**Why it works:** Acknowledges the reader's own skepticism, then overcomes it with a specific result. The timeframe makes it feel real and achievable.

---

### The Review Count

```
This [Product] Has [Number] 5-Star Reviews. [Unexpected Detail] Is Why.
```

**Examples:**
- "This Mattress Has 14,000 5-Star Reviews. The 'Cool Side' Is Why."
- "This Kibble Has 47,000 5-Star Reviews. The Smell Test Is Why."

**Why it works:** The number is social proof. The unexpected detail is the curiosity gap. Together they pre-sell before the click.

---

### The Crowd Proof

```
[Number] [Customers] Can't Be Wrong. Here's Why [Product] Is Selling Out.
```

**Examples:**
- "47,000 Dog Owners Can't Be Wrong. Here's Why This Kibble Is Selling Out."
- "200,000 Homeowners Can't Be Wrong. Here's Why This Insulation Is Selling Out."

**Why it works:** Volume = validation. "Selling Out" = urgency. The reader feels late to a trend.

## Identity Filtering Formulas

### The Geo-Targeted Callout

```
[Demographic] In [Location] [Urgency Statement]
```

**Examples:**
- "Senior Drivers In Idaho Did Not See This Coming"
- "Homeowners In Texas Born Before 1965 Should Check This"

**Why it works:** Disqualifies 99% of people scrolling, which is exactly the point. If you're in that group, the ad just called your name in a crowded room.

---

### The Condition Callout

```
Anyone With [Condition] Should [Action] This
```

**Examples:**
- "Anyone With Diabetes Should Watch This"
- "Anyone Over 60 With Stiff Joints Should Read This Before Bed"
- "Attention, diabetics! Do this before your levels go below 4.9"

**Why it works:** If that's you, you physically cannot scroll past. If it's not, you were never the customer anyway. Keeps CPCs low and intent high.

---

### The Accepted Normal

Call out a problem people have stopped questioning. Tell them it doesn't have to be that way.

```
[Specific Group] With [Problem They Accepted]: [Few/Nobody] Know(s) This [Simple Fix]
```

**Examples:**
- "Side sleepers with achy shoulders: few know this trick"
- "Stay-at-home moms in [City] are convinced that this drink finally stops overeating"

**Why it works:** Targets a problem people accepted as just how life is. "Achy shoulders" is something side sleepers live with — they never thought to fix it. The ad tells them it doesn't have to be this way. "Few know" creates exclusivity.

## Third-Person Story Formulas

### The Someone Else's Result

Tell a story about another person. No commands. No promises. Just a result the reader wants.

```
This [simple method] made [Name] [achieve result] in [timeframe]
```

**Examples:**
- "This new method made Evelyn stop chewing her nails in less than 30 days"
- "'It didn't fit,' Michael (53) shares the secret how women started paying more attention to him again"
- "The potato challenge: Nutritionist helps couple lose 30lbs in 2 months with 5 min ritual"

**Why it works:** Stories lower resistance. We lean in instead of defend. If someone like me did it, why can't I?

---

### The Implied Transformation

Don't state the outcome. Describe a symptom of the outcome and let the brain fill in the rest.

**Examples:**
- "Clothes don't fit the same" (never says she lost weight)
- "Women started paying more attention" (never says he got attractive)
- "Hollywood uses this since 2004" (implies the secret behind a visible result)

**Why it works:** The transformation the reader imagines is always more powerful than the one you state. Their brain fills in their personal version of the dream outcome.

## Compound Formulas

These templates stack multiple psychological principles into a single headline.

### The Post-Failure Reveal

Authority + contrarian + curiosity. For audiences who've already tried solutions that didn't work.

```
[Authority] reveals: The REAL reason [problem] affects you even after [other solution]
```

**Examples:**
- "Neurologist reveals: The REAL reason your migraines persist even after medication"
- "Pharmacist explains: The reason why most anti-migraine solutions don't work long term"

**Why it works:** Validates their failure (you were right, it didn't work), blames a hidden cause (it's not your fault), and borrows authority. The reader who's tried everything and given up is suddenly paying attention again.

---

### The Objection Killer

Third-person story + tiny ask + objection removal. Removes the reader's biggest excuse before they even raise it.

```
[Problem] and nothing seems to work? See how [Name] solved [problem] in under [timeframe], without [objection]
```

**Examples:**
- "Back pain and nothing seems to work? See how Sandra solved her chronic pain in under 28 days, without surgery"
- "Can't lose weight and nothing seems to work? See how David dropped 22lbs in 6 weeks, without giving up carbs"

**Why it works:** "Nothing seems to work" validates their frustration. A named person creates third-person storytelling. The timeframe promises speed. "Without [objection]" removes the barrier they're already thinking about.

---

### The Kitchen Secret

Mystery + comparison + authority confusion. The solution is absurdly ordinary, which makes it irresistible.

```
Doctors can't explain why this kitchen secret [solves problem] faster than [different solution]
```

**Examples:**
- "Doctors can't explain why this kitchen secret cuts weight 4X faster than keto"
- "Doctors can't explain why this kitchen ingredient relieves joint pain faster than prescription meds"

**Why it works:** "Kitchen secret" is disarming — it's not a supplement or a program, it's something already in your house. "Doctors can't explain" borrows authority while creating mystery. The comparison to a known solution gives scale.

---

### The Societal Norm Challenge

Authority + contrarian + identity. Challenges something society accepts as normal.

```
Society thinks it's normal: [Authority] begs people to stop [societal norm] when you have [problem]
```

**Examples:**
- "Society thinks it's normal: Cardiologist begs people to stop eating breakfast cereal when you have high cholesterol"
- "Society thinks it's normal: Dermatologist begs people to stop using this soap when you have eczema"

**Why it works:** "Society thinks it's normal" implies everyone is wrong and you're about to discover the truth. "Begs" from an authority creates urgency and personal concern. Challenges a behavior the reader does daily without thinking.

---

### The Enemy Identified

Enemy framing + discovery + finality. Declares the villain has been found.

```
The real enemy of [problem] has been identified!
```

**Examples:**
- "The real enemy of weight loss has been identified!"
- "The real enemy of deep sleep has been identified!"

**Why it works:** "Real enemy" implies everything you blamed before was wrong. "Has been identified" feels like breaking news — a discovery just happened and you need to know. Short, punchy, impossible to scroll past.

---

## Fear + Discovery Formulas

### The Exposure

```
[Number] [Timeframe] [Pain] Exposed: The [Mechanism] Fix
```

**Examples:**
- "30-Year Mortgage Trap Exposed: The Equity Hack Fix"
- "20-Year Joint Pain Cycle Exposed: The Inflammation Fix"

**Why it works:** "Exposed" implies hidden truth. Long timeframe validates the reader's frustration. "Fix" promises resolution.

---

### The Tribal Opportunity

```
[Demographic] Should Take Advantage of [Scheme/Program/Method]
```

**Examples:**
- "British Homeowners Should Take Advantage of This Insulation Scheme"
- "Veterans Over 55 Should Take Advantage of This New Benefit"

**Why it works:** Exclusivity framing — there's something on the table you're not grabbing. Others are already getting it. You're being left behind. FOMO meets identity.
